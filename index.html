U.S. County-Level Drought Hotspot Analysis

GeoPandas Spatial Autocorrelation Moran's I LISA Bokeh

This project identifies county-level drought hotspots across the contiguous United States using U.S. Drought Monitor data (May 26, 2026). Area-weighted drought severity was calculated for all 3,109 CONUS counties, followed by global and local spatial autocorrelation analysis.

  • 2,422 out of 3,109 counties had measurable drought coverage
  • Moran's I = 0.9123 (p = 0.001) — very strong spatial clustering
  • 872 High-High drought hotspot counties identified in the West and Southeast
  • Highest severity in southern Georgia and northern Florida (DM = 4.0)

Impact of Impervious Surface on Flood Susceptibility

Rasterio NLCD 2023 SVI GeoPandas Matplotlib

This project analyzes the impact of impervious surfaces on flood susceptibility across Cook, DuPage, and DeKalb counties in Illinois using NLCD 2023 fractional impervious surface data. Social vulnerability (SVI) is incorporated at the census tract level to examine whether more vulnerable communities face higher flood risk.

  • Clear urban-rural gradient: Cook and DuPage higher than DeKalb
  • Correlation between SVI and impervious surface: r = 0.344
  • More vulnerable communities tend to be in more impervious areas
  • Combined imperviousness and vulnerability highlights flood risk areas
